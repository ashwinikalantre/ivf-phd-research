# Economic and Access Context

IVF is not only a medical treatment. It is also a financial and access issue.

## Main Point

The cost of IVF affects who can start treatment, how many cycles they can attempt, and whether they stop after failure.

## India Cost Evidence

HTAIn/ICMR-NIRRCH reported:

| Cost Indicator | Value |
| --- | ---: |
| Average IVF out-of-pocket expenditure in public hospitals | Rs. 1,10,104 |
| Average IVF out-of-pocket expenditure in private hospitals | Rs. 2,37,851 |
| Catastrophic health expenditure for IVF | 85% |
| Direct health costs share of OOPE | 44% |

Source:

- [HTAIn Policy Brief on IVF cost](https://htain.dhr.gov.in/images/pdf/Policy_Briefs/79.Policy_Brief_IVF.pdf)

## What This Means

Because IVF is costly:

- failed cycles have high emotional and financial impact
- couples may not afford many attempts
- counseling should be realistic and personalized
- decision support can help explain risk and expectation

## Social Class and Access

Faculty may ask:

> Which social class can afford IVF?

Careful answer:

IVF access is strongly affected by affordability. In India, private IVF cost can be high relative to household income, so access is easier for urban and economically stronger groups. Lower-income couples may delay treatment, stop early, or never access ART.

Use this carefully. Do not make unsupported claims about exact class distribution unless you have a direct dataset.

Supporting source:

- [Financial costs of ART in low- and middle-income countries](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10029849/)
