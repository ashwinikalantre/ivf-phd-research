# IVF Success and Age

Age is one of the most important factors in IVF outcome prediction.

## Main Point

IVF success generally declines with female age, especially after the late 30s and early 40s.

This supports the need for personalized prediction because two patients with different age, AMH, embryo quality and diagnosis can have very different chances.

## Evidence

HFEA 2023 UK data reported:

| Age Group / Measure | Value |
| --- | ---: |
| Fresh embryo-transfer pregnancy rate, all ages | 31% |
| Fresh embryo-transfer pregnancy rate, age 18-34 | 41% |
| Fresh embryo-transfer birth rate, age 18-34 | 35% |
| Fresh embryo-transfer birth rate, age 43-44 | 5% |

Source:

- [HFEA Fertility treatment 2023 trends and figures](https://www.hfea.gov.uk/about-us/publications/research-and-data/fertility-treatment-2023-trends-and-figures/)

## How to Answer Panel Question

If asked:

> Why age is important in IVF?

Answer:

Age affects ovarian reserve, oocyte quality, embryo quality and miscarriage risk. That is why age repeatedly appears as an important predictor in IVF outcome studies.

## Link to Our Research

The model should not use age alone.

It should combine age with:

- AMH
- AFC
- diagnosis
- stimulation response
- embryo quality
- endometrial thickness
- previous IVF history

This is why a personalized model is useful.
