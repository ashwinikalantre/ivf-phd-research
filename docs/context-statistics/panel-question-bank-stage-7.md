# Panel Defense Script

This page converts the preparation into speaking scripts for the faculty panel.

The aim is to sound clear, honest and research-ready, without pretending that the final title or dataset is already fixed.


## 30-Second Topic Introduction

My broad area is women's healthcare, with a primary focus on IVF.

I reviewed recent AI and IVF literature to identify repeated gaps, rather than fixing a title too early. The current working direction is an explainable and personalized clinical decision-support framework for IVF outcome prediction.

The final title will depend on clinic data access, especially whether clinical, embryology, lifestyle and external validation data are available.

## 2-Minute Overview

My research started from the broad area of women's healthcare and IVF. IVF treatment involves many clinical decisions, such as ovarian stimulation, embryo assessment, embryo transfer and outcome counseling.

I reviewed recent papers from 2021 to 2025 covering AI in IVF, machine learning, explainable AI, clinical decision support, personalized treatment and predictive analytics.

The literature shows that many models already exist for IVF outcome prediction, embryo selection, ovarian stimulation and post-transfer risk. So my focus is not simply to build another prediction model.

The repeated gaps are external validation, single-center data, limited clinical decision-support translation, limited real-world deployment evidence, and limited clinician-tested explainability.

Therefore, my current direction is to develop an explainable and personalized decision-support framework for IVF outcome prediction. But this is still provisional. If embryo data is not available, the title will be reduced to clinical-data-based prediction. If lifestyle data is available, it can become a personalization component. If more than one clinic is available, external validation can be included.

The immediate next step is to confirm dataset feasibility with doctors and clinics.

## 5-Minute Overview

Good morning respected faculty members.

My broad research domain is women's healthcare, and my primary interest is IVF. I have not finalized the PhD title prematurely because the correct title should emerge from literature gaps and dataset feasibility.

I followed a structured process: broad area, literature review, existing work, limitations, repeated gaps, research problem, research questions, objectives and then topic direction.

The literature review currently includes 35 paper notes covering IVF outcome prediction, embryo selection, ovarian stimulation decision support, FET/endometrium prediction, explainable AI, trustworthy AI and clinician adoption.

The literature shows that AI in IVF is an active field. Studies use logistic regression, Random Forest, XGBoost, LightGBM, deep learning and SHAP-based explainability. Some papers focus on live birth, some on clinical pregnancy, some on embryo selection and some on stimulation decisions.

However, repeated gaps remain. Many studies are retrospective or single-center. Many lack external validation. Some use explainability, but clinician-tested usefulness is still limited. Many studies stop at model performance and do not become real clinical decision-support systems.

So the current research problem is: IVF prediction models may show technical performance, but they often remain model-centric and are not always explainable, externally validated, personalized or clinician-usable.

My working direction is an explainable and personalized clinical decision-support framework for IVF outcome prediction using available clinical and embryological data.

This direction is conditional on data. If only clinical data is available, the study becomes clinical-data-based explainable prediction. If embryology data is available, multimodal clinical and embryology prediction can be tested. If lifestyle data is available, lifestyle personalization can be added. If another clinic or independent time period is available, external or temporal validation can be performed.

The methodology will start with data feasibility and ethics approval, not model building. After anonymized data collection, I will perform data cleaning, missing-value analysis, outcome definition checking, exploratory analysis, baseline logistic regression, ML model comparison, calibration, validation and explainable AI.

The model output should support doctors with predicted probability, important positive and negative factors and patient-level explanations. It will not replace clinicians or decide treatment.

The next practical step is clinic discussion: what data exists, what outcome is recorded, whether embryo and lifestyle variables are available, whether more than one clinic is possible, and whether doctors can review explanation outputs.

## Difficult Questions And Safe Answers

### Is this topic already done?

Safe answer:

> Prediction in IVF is already studied, so simple prediction alone is not enough. The gap I am focusing on is explainable, validated and clinician-usable decision support. The final novelty will depend on available Indian clinic data, validation design and clinician review feasibility.

### Why not finalize the title now?

Safe answer:

> Because the title must match the dataset. If embryo data is unavailable, I should not keep "embryological data" in the title. If live birth is unavailable, I should not claim live-birth prediction. The responsible approach is to finalize the title after clinic data confirmation.

### What if doctors do not give data?

Safe answer:

> Then model development cannot start. The immediate task is clinic discussion, ethics approval and anonymized data access. Until then, the work remains literature-backed proposal development.

### What if only one clinic gives data?

Safe answer:

> The study can still be feasible as a single-center study with internal or temporal validation. But I will not claim external validation or broad generalization.

### What if live birth is not recorded?

Safe answer:

> Clinical pregnancy can be used as the dependent variable, but it must be clearly stated as a limitation because clinical pregnancy is not the same as live birth.

### Why not use deep learning?

Safe answer:

> Deep learning is suitable for image, video or very large datasets. If the available data is tabular clinical data, logistic regression, Random Forest, XGBoost and LightGBM are more realistic and defensible.

### Why is logistic regression included?

Safe answer:

> Logistic regression is a transparent baseline. Some reviewed studies show that simpler models can perform similarly to complex ML. A PhD model should be clinically useful, not complex only for novelty.

### Why XAI?

Safe answer:

> IVF counseling is sensitive. Doctors need to understand why a model gives a risk estimate. XAI can show patient-level factors, but it explains model behavior, not causality.

### Can your model improve IVF success?

Safe answer:

> I cannot claim that at this point. Improving success would require prospective clinical testing. The current aim is prediction, explanation, validation and decision-support design.

### What is the role of doctors?

Safe answer:

> Doctors remain central. The system is intended to support counseling and risk understanding, not to decide treatment or replace clinical judgment.

### Why Indian data?

Safe answer:

> Many IVF-AI studies use international or single-center datasets. Indian validation evidence is limited, though not completely absent. Indian clinic data can help test local applicability, but I will not claim national generalization unless the data supports it.

### What is the minimum feasible PhD?

Safe answer:

> The minimum feasible PhD is an explainable clinical-data-based IVF outcome prediction study using reliable outcome data, careful validation and clear limitations.

### What is the best-case PhD?

Safe answer:

> The best-case PhD would use clinical, embryology and lifestyle data, include external validation from another clinic or source, and include clinician review of explanation outputs.
