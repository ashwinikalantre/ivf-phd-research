# Panel Preparation Workflow

This page is the entry point for faculty panel preparation.

The goal is to move from broad questions to polished spoken answers without guessing, overclaiming or finalizing the title too early.

## Preparation Principle

Every panel answer should fall into one of three categories:

| Category | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Confident | Supported by reviewed papers, statistics pages or reliable sources. |
| Conditional | Depends on clinic data, ethics approval, outcome availability or clinician review. |
| Not yet claimable | Should not be stated as a conclusion at this stage. |

## Final Panel Position

Use this as the central message:

> My final title is not fixed yet. I have identified a strong literature-backed direction: explainable and personalized decision support for IVF outcome prediction. The exact title, outcome, variables, hypotheses and validation strategy will be finalized after clinic data feasibility is confirmed.

## Stop Rules

Stop polishing and move to doctor/clinic discussion when you can answer:

- Why IVF?
- Why AI in IVF?
- Why prediction alone is not enough?
- What are the repeated gaps?
- What data is required?
- What if embryo data is missing?
- What if lifestyle data is missing?
- What if external validation is not possible?
- What hypotheses are only tentative?
- What claims should be avoided?

## Statements To Avoid

| Avoid | Safer Version |
| --- | --- |
| My title is finalized. | My working direction is ready; final title depends on data. |
| AI will improve IVF success. | AI may support prediction and counseling; outcome improvement needs prospective validation. |
| Lifestyle affects IVF outcome in our data. | Lifestyle will be tested only if reliable lifestyle data is available. |
| We will do external validation. | External validation will be done only if independent data is available. |
| XAI will make doctors trust the model. | Clinician trust/usefulness must be evaluated separately. |

## Stage 8 Completion Check

Stage 8 is complete when:

- all panel-preparation pages are easy to find
- the student knows which page to read for which question
- the workflow separates faculty preparation from doctor data discussion
- unsupported claims are blocked clearly
