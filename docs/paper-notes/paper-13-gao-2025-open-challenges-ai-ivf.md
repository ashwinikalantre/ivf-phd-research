## Research Objective

The review aimed to summarize current AI applications in IVF and identify open challenges for applying AI safely and effectively across the IVF cycle.

The paper focuses on how AI can support decision-making throughout IVF while recognizing barriers such as generalizability, bias, transparency and clinical validation.

## Main Findings / Synthesis

The review concludes that AI has strong potential across IVF, but clinical success depends on more than model performance.

Important synthesis points:

- AI can support decision-making throughout IVF.
- Embryo selection remains a major AI application area.
- Multimodal data may improve personalized prediction.
- Federated learning could allow clinics to collaborate without sharing sensitive raw data.
- Digital twins may eventually allow virtual testing of treatment options.
- Limited generalizability, data bias and lack of transparent validation remain major barriers.
- Safe and equitable implementation requires clinical validation, ethical safeguards and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Confidence: **High**
