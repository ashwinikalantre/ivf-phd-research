# Candidate Topics

These are candidate directions, not final titles.

## Candidate 1

**Explainable Multimodal Machine Learning Framework for Personalized IVF Outcome Prediction and Clinical Decision Support**

Strength:

- aligns with repeated gaps
- broad enough for clinical, embryology and lifestyle data
- suitable for Computer Applications

Risk:

- needs data access and careful scope control

## Candidate 2

**Trustworthy AI-Based Clinical Decision Support for Personalized Ovarian Stimulation and IVF Outcome Prediction**

Strength:

- strong clinical decision-support angle
- connects with dose, trigger and stimulation papers

Risk:

- may require more clinical protocol data than outcome-prediction data

## Candidate 3

**Integration of Clinical, Embryological and Lifestyle Data for Explainable IVF Success Prediction**

Strength:

- strong personalization angle
- useful if lifestyle data access is feasible

Risk:

- lifestyle evidence must be strengthened before final title selection

## Candidate 4

**Explainable and Fair Machine Learning Models for IVF Live-Birth Prediction with Indian Population Validation**

Strength:

- strong novelty if Indian validation data is available
- directly addresses external validation and population-diversity gaps

Risk:

- depends heavily on Indian dataset access

## Current Recommendation

After Phase 4, Candidate 1 remains the strongest direction, but it should be sharpened.

## Strongest Working Title

**An Explainable and Personalized Clinical Decision Support Framework for IVF Outcome Prediction Using Multimodal Clinical and Embryological Data**

## Stronger If Indian Data Is Feasible

**An Explainable and Personalized Clinical Decision Support Framework for IVF Outcome Prediction Using Multimodal Clinical and Embryological Data in Indian Women**

## Stronger If Lifestyle Data Is Feasible

**Explainable Multimodal AI for Personalized IVF Outcome Prediction Using Clinical, Embryological and Lifestyle Data**

## Supervisor Caution

Do not put `Indian women` or `lifestyle data` in the final title unless data access is confirmed.

The safest evidence-based title at this stage is the first one.
