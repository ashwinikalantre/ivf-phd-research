# Proposal Structure

This page provides the current proposal skeleton.

## Proposed Title

**An Explainable and Personalized Clinical Decision Support Framework for IVF Outcome Prediction Using Multimodal Clinical and Embryological Data**

## Background

In vitro fertilization is a major assisted reproductive technology, but success rates remain variable and emotionally, physically and financially demanding for couples. Recent studies show that artificial intelligence and machine learning can support embryo selection, ovarian stimulation, pregnancy prediction, live-birth prediction and treatment personalization.

However, the literature also shows repeated limitations: many models are retrospective, single-center, insufficiently externally validated, weakly explainable and not translated into clinically usable decision-support workflows.

## Research Gap

The repeated gaps are:

- lack of external validation and generalizability
- lack of explainable and trustworthy AI
- lack of clinical decision-support translation
- limited multimodal personalization
- limited Indian/contextual validation
- limited real-world usability evaluation

## Research Problem

Current AI models in IVF can predict outcomes, but many are not explainable, externally validated, personalized or clinically usable. There is a need for an explainable and personalized decision-support framework that integrates multimodal IVF data and supports clinician-patient counseling without replacing clinical judgment.

## Research Questions

1. Which clinical, embryological, imaging and contextual factors are most useful for personalized IVF outcome prediction?
2. Can a multimodal ML model improve IVF outcome prediction compared with conventional or single-modality models?
3. How can XAI methods provide clinician-usable and patient-understandable explanations for IVF predictions?
4. How well does the proposed model generalize across patient subgroups, clinics or Indian population data?
5. How can predictive outputs be translated into a clinical decision-support workflow without replacing clinician judgment?

## Objectives

1. To conduct a structured literature review of AI, ML, XAI and CDSS applications in IVF.
2. To identify repeated gaps in IVF AI research using a literature review matrix and gap-frequency analysis.
3. To design a multimodal IVF outcome prediction framework using clinical and embryological data.
4. To integrate explainable AI techniques for identifying patient-specific outcome drivers.
5. To evaluate the model using predictive performance, calibration, subgroup behavior and generalizability.
6. To propose a clinician-facing decision-support workflow for personalized IVF counseling.

## Expected Contribution

The expected contribution is not only a prediction model.

The contribution is a framework that combines:

- multimodal IVF outcome prediction
- explainability
- personalized decision support
- validation-aware evaluation
- clinical usability thinking

## Scope

In scope:

- IVF outcome prediction
- clinical pregnancy/live-birth related outcomes
- clinical and embryological variables
- explainable ML
- decision-support framework design
- validation and subgroup analysis

Conditionally in scope:

- Indian population validation
- lifestyle variables
- ultrasound/radiomics variables

Out of scope:

- replacing clinician decisions
- making treatment decisions without clinician review
- purely biological IVF mechanisms
- genetic embryo selection ethics beyond the decision-support scope
