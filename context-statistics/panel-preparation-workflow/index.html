    Panel Preparation Workflow¶

    This page is the entry point for faculty panel preparation.

    The goal is to move from broad questions to polished spoken answers without guessing, overclaiming or finalizing the title too early.

    Preparation Principle¶

    Every panel answer should fall into one of three categories:

    Category Meaning
    Confident Supported by reviewed papers, statistics pages or reliable sources.
    Conditional Depends on clinic data, ethics approval, outcome availability or clinician review.
    Not yet claimable Should not be stated as a conclusion at this point.

    Recommended Reading Order¶

    Step Page Use
    1 Panel Question Areas See the full set of possible faculty questions.
    2 Evidence-Based Panel Answers Learn safe answers with confidence labels.
    3 Dataset Feasibility Plan Prepare fallback answers for dataset limitations.
    4 Variable and Outcome Map Understand dependent variables, predictors and claim boundaries.
    5 Research Questions and Hypotheses Use only testable, dataset-dependent hypotheses.
    6 Methodology Blueprint Explain how the study will be conducted.
    7 Panel Defense Script Practice 30-second, 2-minute and 5-minute answers.
    8 Quick Panel Question Bank Use before the actual meeting for compact revision.
    9 Final Claim Audit Check unsupported claims, stale numbers and title wording.

    What Each Page Solves¶

    Page Main Problem It Solves
    Panel Question Areas What questions can faculty ask?
    Evidence-Based Panel Answers How do I answer safely without assumptions?
    Dataset Feasibility Plan What if I do not get all data?
    Variable and Outcome Map Which variables matter, and what can I claim from them?
    Research Questions and Hypotheses Which research questions and hypotheses are testable?
    Methodology Blueprint What is the methodology?
    Panel Defense Script How do I speak clearly in the panel?
    Final Claim Audit What should I re-check or avoid before presenting?

    Final Panel Position¶

    Use this as the central message:

    My final title is not fixed yet. I have identified a strong literature-backed direction: explainable and personalized decision support for IVF outcome prediction. The exact title, outcome, variables, hypotheses and validation strategy will be finalized after clinic data feasibility is confirmed.

    What Is Ready Now¶

    Item Status
    Broad domain Ready
    Literature direction Ready
    Repeated gap explanation Ready
    Dataset feasibility logic Ready
    Variable map Ready
    Tentative research questions Ready but conditional
    Tentative hypotheses Ready but conditional
    Methodology blueprint Ready but conditional
    Final title Not fixed
    Final dataset Not confirmed
    Final hypotheses Not fixed

    When To Stop Polishing¶

    Stop polishing and move to doctor/clinic discussion when you can answer:

    • Why IVF?
    • Why AI in IVF?
    • Why prediction alone is not enough?
    • What are the repeated gaps?
    • What data is required?
    • What if embryo data is missing?
    • What if lifestyle data is missing?
    • What if external validation is not possible?
    • What hypotheses are only tentative?
    • What claims should be avoided?

    Before Meeting Faculty¶

    Read in this order:

    1. Panel Defense Script for spoken answers.
    2. Evidence-Based Panel Answers for safe evidence-backed answers.
    3. Dataset Feasibility Plan for feasibility questions.
    4. Variable and Outcome Map for variable questions.
    5. Quick panel question bank for revision.

    Before Meeting Doctors¶

    Read in this order:

    1. Dataset Feasibility Plan for data feasibility scenarios.
    2. Variable and Outcome Map for variable and outcome mapping.
    3. Data Collection Framework.
    4. Clinic Discussion Checklist.

    Statements To Avoid¶

    Avoid Safer Version
    My title is finalized. My working direction is ready; final title depends on data.
    AI will improve IVF success. AI may support prediction and counseling; outcome improvement needs prospective validation.
    Lifestyle affects IVF outcome in our data. Lifestyle will be tested only if reliable lifestyle data is available.
    We will do external validation. External validation will be done only if independent data is available.
    XAI will make doctors trust the model. Clinician trust/usefulness must be evaluated separately.
