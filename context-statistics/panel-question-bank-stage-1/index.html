    Panel Question Bank Stage 1¶

    This page is the first-stage question skeleton for panel preparation.

    At this stage, the purpose is not to give final answers. The purpose is to collect realistic questions that faculty may ask and organize them into clear categories.

    Stage 1 Rule¶

    Do not answer from assumption.

    Later stages should answer each question using:

    • evidence from reviewed papers
    • reliable statistics pages
    • dataset-feasibility status
    • confidence label: Confident, Conditional, or Not yet claimable

    1. Domain Understanding¶

    No Question
    1 What is IVF?
    2 What is ART?
    3 Why is IVF outcome prediction important?
    4 What is the difference between clinical pregnancy and live birth?
    5 Why is live birth considered a stronger outcome?
    6 What are common reasons for IVF failure?
    7 How does female age affect IVF success?
    8 What is the role of embryo quality in IVF?
    9 What is ovarian stimulation?
    10 What is the difference between fresh and frozen embryo transfer?

    2. Literature Review¶

    No Question
    11 How many papers have you reviewed?
    12 Why did you select papers from 2021-2025?
    13 What are the major themes found in the literature?
    14 Which papers are strongest evidence?
    15 Which papers are weaker or only supporting evidence?
    16 What repeated gaps are found across papers?
    17 Are most studies retrospective or prospective?
    18 Do studies use live birth or only clinical pregnancy?
    19 How many papers include explainable AI?
    20 How many papers include Indian population data?

    3. Research Gap¶

    No Question
    21 What is the main research gap?
    22 Why is external validation important?
    23 Why is single-center data a limitation?
    24 What is missing in current IVF-AI studies?
    25 Is lack of lifestyle data a real repeated gap?
    26 Is lack of Indian population data a repeated gap?
    27 Is explainability still missing?
    28 Is clinical decision support missing?
    29 Are current AI models clinically deployed?
    30 What gap is strong enough for a PhD?

    4. Dataset Feasibility¶

    No Question
    31 What dataset do you need?
    32 From where will you collect IVF data?
    33 What if clinics do not share data?
    34 What if only one clinic gives data?
    35 What if live birth data is unavailable?
    36 What if embryo variables are unavailable?
    37 What if lifestyle variables are unavailable?
    38 What if sample size is small?
    39 What is the minimum viable dataset?
    40 What is the ideal dataset?

    5. Feasible Study Design¶

    No Question
    41 Can this PhD be done without external validation?
    42 Can it be done without deployment in clinics?
    43 Can it be done using retrospective data?
    44 Can it be done using anonymized Excel records?
    45 What is the fallback if only clinical data is available?
    46 What is the fallback if embryo data is available?
    47 What is the fallback if lifestyle data is available?
    48 What if doctors only allow clinician feedback, not deployment?
    49 How will the scope remain manageable?
    50 What will be excluded from the PhD?

    6. Variables¶

    No Question
    51 What is the dependent variable?
    52 What are independent variables?
    53 What are confounding variables?
    54 What are moderator variables?
    55 What are mediator variables?
    56 Which variables are clinical?
    57 Which variables are embryological?
    58 Which variables are lifestyle-related?
    59 Which variables are demographic?
    60 Which variables are treatment-cycle variables?

    7. Hypotheses¶

    No Question
    61 What is your main hypothesis?
    62 Are hypotheses finalized?
    63 Which hypotheses depend on embryo data?
    64 Which hypotheses depend on lifestyle data?
    65 Which hypotheses depend on external validation?
    66 How will you test whether multimodal data improves prediction?
    67 How will you test whether XAI improves clinician understanding?
    68 Can you hypothesize improved live birth?
    69 What null hypotheses are possible?
    70 What if hypotheses cannot be tested due to missing data?

    8. Methodology¶

    No Question
    71 Which ML models will you use?
    72 Why not use only deep learning?
    73 Why compare with logistic regression?
    74 How will missing data be handled?
    75 How will class imbalance be handled?
    76 What validation strategy will be used?
    77 What metrics will be reported?
    78 How will explainability be implemented?
    79 How will model calibration be checked?
    80 How will overfitting be avoided?

    9. Statistics¶

    No Question
    81 What descriptive statistics will be used?
    82 Which statistical tests are suitable?
    83 Will you use chi-square test?
    84 Will you use t-test or Mann-Whitney test?
    85 Will you use logistic regression?
    86 Will you report confidence intervals?
    87 Will you calculate sample size?
    88 Will you perform power analysis?
    89 How will AUC be interpreted?
    90 Why is accuracy alone not enough?

    10. Ethics And Privacy¶

    No Question
    91 How will patient data be anonymized?
    92 Is ethics approval required?
    93 How will embryo-related data be handled?
    94 How will sensitive fertility data be protected?
    95 Can AI create bias in IVF decisions?
    96 How will clinician oversight be maintained?
    97 What should AI not decide?
    98 How will patient trust be protected?
    99 What are the risks of over-reliance on AI?
    100 How will responsible AI be ensured?

    11. Novelty And Contribution¶

    No Question
    101 What is new in your work?
    102 How is this different from existing IVF-AI papers?
    103 Is XAI alone enough for novelty?
    104 Is Indian dataset enough for novelty?
    105 Is lifestyle integration novel?
    106 Is CDSS the main contribution?
    107 What is the academic contribution?
    108 What is the clinical contribution?
    109 What is the technical contribution?
    110 What is the societal contribution?

    12. Limitations¶

    No Question
    111 What are expected limitations?
    112 What if the model does not perform well?
    113 What if external validation is not possible?
    114 What if data is biased?
    115 What if doctors do not trust explanations?
    116 What if lifestyle data is self-reported?
    117 What if live birth follow-up is incomplete?
    118 What if the dataset is too small for deep learning?
    119 What claims will you avoid?
    120 What must be confirmed before finalizing the title?

    Stage 1 Completion Check¶

    This stage is complete when:

    • the question list covers the likely panel areas
    • duplicate questions are removed
    • the highest-priority questions are marked for Stage 2
    • no answers are written without evidence

    Next Stage¶

    Stage 2 will convert selected questions into safe answers using this structure:

    Field Purpose
    Question The panel question
    Likely concern What the faculty member is really testing
    Safe answer Short, defensible answer
    Evidence basis Paper numbers, statistics pages or source pages
    Confidence Confident, Conditional or Not yet claimable
    If challenged What to say if faculty pushes deeper
