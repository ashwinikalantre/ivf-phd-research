    Paper 13: Current Progress and Open Challenges for AI Across the IVF Cycle¶

    Citation¶

    Gao, Y., Yuan, Y., Wang, K., Wang, Y., Gao, T., Yang, Y., Ma, L.-S., Li, R., Wang, G., & Liu, X. (2025). Current progress and open challenges for applying artificial intelligence across the in vitro fertilization cycle. Patterns, 6(11), Article 101347.

    DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.patter.2025.101347

    Sources checked:

    • Cell / Patterns article page
    • PubMed record
    • Publisher PDF metadata/search extract

    Access note:

    The full Cell page/PDF was partially restricted during extraction, but publisher metadata, PubMed metadata and indexed abstract text confirm the article type, title, authors, journal, DOI and core review claims. This note is therefore written as a review-level synthesis, not a detailed primary-study extraction.

    Related study terms:

    • Multimodal learning
    • Federated learning
    • Digital twin
    • Synthetic data
    • Trustworthy AI
    • Model generalizability
    • Data bias
    • Clinical validation

    Why This Paper Matters¶

    This paper is useful because it reviews AI applications across the entire IVF cycle and organizes evidence by data modality.

    It supports our PhD direction because our proposed work also depends on:

    • clinical data
    • embryology data
    • image/video data if available
    • multimodal integration
    • explainability
    • validation
    • clinical implementation

    The paper is especially useful for explaining why the field is moving from isolated AI models toward more integrated, validated and trustworthy AI systems.

    Research Objective¶

    The review aimed to summarize current AI applications in IVF and identify open challenges for applying AI safely and effectively across the IVF cycle.

    The paper focuses on how AI can support decision-making throughout IVF while recognizing barriers such as generalizability, bias, transparency and clinical validation.

    Study Design and Dataset¶

    Item Extracted Information
    Article type Review article
    Journal Patterns
    Publication year 2025
    Dataset Not applicable; no original dataset generated
    Review organization AI studies organized by data modality and IVF-cycle stage
    Main focus Current applications and open challenges for AI across IVF

    Confidence: High for article type and metadata; Medium/High for detailed review scope due to partial full-text access.

    Review Scope¶

    The review discusses AI applications using different IVF data modalities, including:

    • structured clinical data
    • embryo images
    • time-lapse videos
    • molecular or omics data
    • multimodal datasets
    • digital-twin concepts
    • federated learning for multi-clinic collaboration
    • explainable and transparent AI
    • ethical and equitable implementation

    Confidence: Medium/High

    Key Arguments¶

    Argument Meaning For Our PhD
    IVF success still depends partly on subjective judgment and labor-intensive workflows AI can support standardization and reproducibility.
    AI can learn from clinical histories, images and molecular traces Multimodal data integration is an important future direction.
    Generalizability remains limited External validation and local validation are necessary.
    Data bias is a major challenge Models trained on narrow populations may not work fairly across settings.
    Transparent AI is essential Explainability and clinician trust are central to adoption.
    Federated learning may help multi-clinic collaboration Privacy-preserving validation may be useful when raw data cannot be shared.
    Ethical safeguards are required AI in reproductive medicine affects sensitive decisions and must be governed carefully.

    Confidence: High

    Methods / Algorithms Discussed¶

    Because this is a review, it does not build one model.

    It discusses AI methods and concepts across IVF applications, including:

    • machine learning on structured clinical data
    • computer vision for embryo/gamete assessment
    • deep learning on images and time-lapse videos
    • multimodal AI
    • federated learning
    • synthetic data
    • digital twins
    • explainable and transparent AI systems

    Confidence: Medium/High

    Main Findings / Synthesis¶

    The review concludes that AI has strong potential across IVF, but clinical success depends on more than model performance.

    Important synthesis points:

    • AI can support decision-making throughout IVF.
    • Embryo selection remains a major AI application area.
    • Multimodal data may improve personalized prediction.
    • Federated learning could allow clinics to collaborate without sharing sensitive raw data.
    • Digital twins may eventually allow virtual testing of treatment options.
    • Limited generalizability, data bias and lack of transparent validation remain major barriers.
    • Safe and equitable implementation requires clinical validation, ethical safeguards and interdisciplinary collaboration.

    Confidence: High

    Limitations / Cautions Stated By Authors¶

    The review highlights these core challenges:

    Challenge Meaning
    Limited model generalizability Models may not work across clinics, populations or treatment workflows.
    Data bias Training datasets may underrepresent important patient groups.
    Need clinical validation AI tools must be tested in clinically meaningful settings.
    Need transparent systems Black-box models can reduce trust and accountability.
    Ethical safeguards IVF-AI raises privacy, fairness and reproductive decision-making concerns.
    Interdisciplinary implementation Clinicians, AI researchers and ethicists must work together.

    Confidence: High

    Hidden Limitations / Our Critical View¶

    These are our PhD-analysis points.

    Critical Point Why It Matters
    Review-level evidence It supports gap framing, but does not provide one primary dataset or model.
    Future-looking concepts Digital twins and large-scale federated learning are promising but not immediately feasible for every PhD dataset.
    Not India-specific It supports the local-validation argument indirectly, but does not solve Indian validation.
    Broad scope It must be used for synthesis and trend analysis, not as direct evidence for one model outcome.
    Partial full-text access in our extraction Some fine details should be checked again if a complete publisher PDF is available later.

    Confidence: High/Medium

    Future Work Suggested¶

    The paper supports future work in:

    • rigorous clinical validation
    • external and multicenter validation
    • transparent and explainable AI systems
    • federated learning for privacy-preserving collaboration
    • reducing data bias
    • multimodal IVF prediction
    • ethical and equitable implementation
    • interdisciplinary AI-IVF research teams

    Confidence: High

    Gap Contribution¶

    Gap Category Supported? Explanation
    Need external validation Yes Generalizability is a central open challenge.
    Trustworthy AI gap Yes Transparency, ethical safeguards and bias are emphasized.
    Need multimodal data Yes Review organizes studies by modality and discusses multimodal potential.
    Need clinical validation Yes Safe implementation depends on clinical validation.
    Data bias/fairness gap Yes Narrow and biased datasets are identified as a problem.
    Real-world deployment gap Yes Translation needs interdisciplinary implementation and safeguards.
    Indian validation gap Indirect Not India-specific, but strongly supports local validation logic.

    Usefulness For Our PhD¶

    Relevance: Very High for trend and gap synthesis

    This paper is valuable for explaining the current direction of the field.

    It supports the claim that AI-IVF research is moving toward:

    • multimodal models
    • explainability
    • privacy-preserving collaboration
    • external validation
    • ethical deployment
    • clinician trust

    For our PhD, this paper should be used in the literature-review synthesis and gap-analysis sections, not as evidence of one model’s performance.

    How We Can Cite This Paper In Thesis¶

    Thesis-ready sentence:

    Gao et al. (2025) reviewed AI applications across the IVF cycle and emphasized that future progress depends on multimodal data integration, rigorous clinical validation, transparent systems, ethical safeguards and improved generalizability across populations and clinics.

    Gap-support sentence:

    The review supports the need for IVF-AI research that moves beyond isolated retrospective models toward trustworthy, externally validated and clinically implementable decision-support systems.

    What This Paper Does Not Prove¶

    This paper does not prove that:

    • one specific IVF-AI model is clinically effective.
    • multimodal AI always outperforms simpler clinical models.
    • federated learning or digital twins are feasible in every clinic.
    • the reviewed evidence applies directly to Indian IVF populations.
    • ethical and explainability problems are already solved.
